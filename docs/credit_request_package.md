# 3. Facility Structure

The proposed facility combines subscription line support and NAV-based downside monitoring.

## Subscription Support

The subscription component is supported by eligible unfunded investor commitments. Investor eligibility is determined using simulated internal criteria, including investor type, rating assumption, funding reliability, and exclusion of affiliate or ineligible investors.

## NAV Support

The NAV component is supported by the fair value of eligible portfolio investments. The NAV analysis provides secondary credit support and is monitored through a maximum LTV covenant and NAV floor.

## Structural Summary

| Structural Feature | Description |
|---|---|
| Collateral | Uncalled capital commitments, capital call rights, eligible NAV support, collection accounts |
| Borrowing Base | Eligible unfunded commitments multiplied by investor-specific advance rates |
| NAV Test | Facility outstanding divided by eligible NAV |
| Availability | Lesser of facility size, subscription borrowing base, and NAV debt capacity, less outstanding borrowings |
| Reporting | Monthly borrowing base; quarterly NAV; annual audited financials |
| Control Focus | Investor eligibility, NAV movement, LTV, excess availability, and concentration |

# 9. Credit Concerns

## 1. NAV Volatility

Private credit portfolio marks may decline due to borrower underperformance, spread widening, valuation changes, or realized losses.

## 2. LP Funding Risk

The repayment profile relies partly on LPs funding future capital calls. Investor default or delayed funding could weaken liquidity.

## 3. Fund-Level Leverage

Leverage introduces structural complexity and may increase downside risk if NAV declines while utilization increases.

## 4. Investor Concentration

Although concentration tests currently pass, the top five investors represent a meaningful portion of total commitments and should be monitored.

## 5. Documentation and Enforceability Risk

The facility depends on appropriate fund authority, enforceable pledge mechanics, account control arrangements, and completion of closing conditions.
