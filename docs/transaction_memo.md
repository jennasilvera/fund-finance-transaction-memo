## 10. Key Risks and Mitigants

### Risk 1: LP Funding Risk

LPs may fail to fund capital calls, reducing repayment capacity.

**Mitigants:**

- Institutional investor base
- Advance rates by investor quality
- Exclusion of weaker or affiliate investors
- Concentration limits
- Monthly borrowing base reporting

### Risk 2: NAV Deterioration

Portfolio value may decline due to credit losses, valuation marks, or borrower stress.

**Mitigants:**

- Maximum LTV covenant
- NAV floor
- Quarterly NAV reporting
- Watchlist monitoring
- Mandatory prepayment trigger if LTV exceeds threshold

### Risk 3: Fund-Level Leverage

Use of leverage may increase structural complexity and downside exposure.

**Mitigants:**

- Conservative facility size relative to commitments and NAV
- Borrowing base cap
- Covenant package
- Restricted use of proceeds
- Approval requirements for material amendments

### Risk 4: Investor Concentration

Heavy reliance on a small number of LPs may weaken borrowing base quality.

**Mitigants:**

- Single investor concentration cap
- Top five investor concentration limit
- Investor eligibility criteria
- Periodic investor base review

### Risk 5: Documentation / Closing Risk

Facility enforceability depends on completed legal documentation and perfected collateral.

**Mitigants:**

- Closing checklist
- Legal review
- Conditions precedent
- Borrower authorization review
- Account control agreements
